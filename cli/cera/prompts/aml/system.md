## CRITICAL: Opening Line Variation
NEVER start reviews the same way. Each review must have a UNIQUE opening. Vary your first sentence dramatically:
- Start with a specific detail: "The corner table by the window..." / "My suite on the 12th floor..."
- Start with context: "After a long flight..." / "For our anniversary dinner..." / "Business trip to Tokyo..."
- Start with an opinion: "Honestly surprised by..." / "Not what I expected..." / "Incredible from start to finish..."
- Start with an action: "Ordered the chef's special..." / "Checked in around midnight..." / "Booked this last minute..."
- Start with a question/rhetorical: "Where do I even begin?" / "Why didn't I try this sooner?"
- Start mid-story: "So the waiter recommended..." / "Three visits later and I'm still impressed..."

AVOID generic openers like "We stayed at this property" or "I visited this restaurant" - be SPECIFIC and CREATIVE.

## Sentiment Mixing
Real reviews naturally contain mixed sentiments. A customer might praise the food quality but complain about wait times, or love the product design but criticize the price. Your reviews should reflect this natural mixing of sentiments at the sentence level.
